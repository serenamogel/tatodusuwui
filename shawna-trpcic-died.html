    Who was Shawna Trpcic?

    Shawna Trpcic was born in Artesia, California, and had a unique journey to becoming a renowned costume designer. Initially, she had dreams of becoming a truck driver while in high school, but a passionate art teacher encouraged her to explore her artistic talents. This guidance led her to enroll in the Otis College of Art and Design, where she studied fashion. During her senior year, Shawna had the opportunity to work on the team of the famous costume designer Bob Mackie. Her love for science fiction and comic books fueled her interest in costume design, as she saw it as a way to create art for the human body, crafting multidimensional characters.

    Career

    Shawna’s career in costume design began with illustration work for Albert Wolsky on the Oscar-winning film “Bugsy.” She then became his assistant in Barry Levinson’s movie “Toys.” Before long, she was designing costumes for her own projects. Notable among these were her contributions to films like “The Cabin in the Woods” alongside Joss Whedon and “The SpongeBob Movie: Sponge on the Run,” as well as various television shows. Shawna’s journey took an exciting turn when she became the assistant costume designer on the pilot of “Firefly” and later took charge of the show. Her passion for “Firefly” and a fortuitous visit to Skywalker Ranch led to a meeting with George Lucas. Years later, her dream came true when she received the opportunity to design costumes for “The Mandalorian” in the Star Wars universe.

    Shawna’s most significant impact was felt through her extensive work on shows created by Jon Favreau and Dave Filoni within the Star Wars universe. She designed costumes for multiple seasons of “The Mandalorian,” “The Book of Boba Fett,” and most recently, “Ahsoka,” garnering critical acclaim. Her collaboration with Jon and Dave, along with her dedicated costume design team and the talented actors, resulted in memorable costumes and characters. Lucasfilm even established a warehouse in Manhattan Beach to house the countless costumes she created. Her remarkable work earned her multiple Emmy nominations and the Costume Designers Guild Award for Excellence in Sci-Fi/Fantasy Television for “The Book of Boba Fett.” Shawna was a beloved presence at Star Wars Celebrations, where she enjoyed interacting with fans and often served as a judge in cosplay competitions.
