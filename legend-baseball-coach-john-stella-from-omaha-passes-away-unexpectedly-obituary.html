    The Omaha community is deeply saddened by the loss of John Stella, a revered baseball coach and a native of South Omaha. Over his remarkable tenure of 40+ years coaching South Omaha baseball players, Stella profoundly influenced countless lives. His famous saying, “baseball is a simple game; all you have to do is catch and throw the ball,” resonated well beyond the confines of the diamond. For Stella, baseball was more than a sport; it was a powerful tool for sculpting young individuals into principled adults.

    Chris Bober posted on Facebook,

    “Sad to hear one of my favorite coaches passed today. John Stella was a South Omaha legend and I will miss him dearly. I’ll have our annual Christmas Eve shot for you this year coach.”

    His impact extended far beyond athletics, touching the lives of underprivileged children in South Omaha. Stella tirelessly instilled fundamental values like honor, history, diligence, and tradition. He dedicated his life to serving Omaha’s youth, leaving an indelible mark that transcended the boundaries of sports.

    During challenging times, Stella emerged as a beacon of motivation, showcasing how lessons learned on the field could shape broader life trajectories. His influence has become interwoven into the fabric of South Omaha, the state of Nebraska, and the very essence of baseball. His legacy wasn’t driven by personal ambition but by a genuine desire to nurture the potential of every young individual.

    The echoes of victorious cheers, camaraderie among colleagues, and shared memories stand as a testament to Stella’s lifelong commitment—an endeavor dedicated to uplifting others. His impact, far-reaching and profound, will endure beyond his time, leaving an everlasting imprint on the hearts and minds of those he touched.
